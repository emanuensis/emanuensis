These alternatives give a glimpse towards the future of a build at 4323 5th NW. The graphic can outline (using the controls on the left) a community, an individual and a limited build; and show for them their aerial imagery and survey data.

Plan can be (<)decreased, reset(/), increased(>) or doubled(d) in size. (or use keystrokes)


A community house build assumes that negotiations between the title insurance company (Stewart) and the Westmorelands succeeds. The fall back is an individual build centered on the "grandfathered" structure i lived in for 15 years and is seen in these 2009 aerial photographs. The land for this fall back position would still need to be settled before a build can proceed.

The LBA survey (below) was drafted to split the 4323 property in half giving Heil the 4325 land + 16 ft of 4323 and the Westmorelands 4317 + 16 ft of 4323. Currently Roy Westmoreland has disavowed the LBA survey and is negotiating for more land.

The plan shown above gives them 5 more feet, reducing Heil`s land to 43x91 feet (from 48x91 in the survey). It also provides for and easement, on the westmoreland land of 8 feet, for roadway usage only. It would provide a buffer and give the synergistic effect of three lanes for vehicle: the middle (being clear) can access either side.

An alternative, without an easement, of 46x91 which makes the two offspring lots of equal size would also work - but would lose the synergy.
